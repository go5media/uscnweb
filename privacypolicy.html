Privacy Policy
Last Updated: 02/08/2025

Start

At the UK Safety Camera Network (USCN), we take privacy seriously. We are committed to operating our camera systems and website in a way that is ethical, transparent, and fully compliant with UK data protection laws.

This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

1. Who We Are

The UK Safety Camera Network (USCN) installs and manages public-facing CCTV cameras in partnership with community hosts. Our goal is to deter crime, promote safety, and provide a transparent and ethical way to access CCTV footage when needed.

Data Controller: UK Safety Camera Network

Email: info@uscn.uk

Support: supportteam@uscn.uk

Phone: 0330 043 9985

2. What Personal Data We Collect

We may collect personal data in the following ways:

A. From Our Cameras
Visual recordings of individuals in public areas

Detection metadata (e.g., motion or facial detection triggers)

Cameras are positioned to avoid capturing private property, and privacy masking is applied where necessary.

B. When You Contact Us
Name, email address, and phone number

Your message or enquiry

Any documents you provide

C. When You Submit a DSAR
Proof of identity (e.g., passport)

Time and location of the footage you're requesting

Any follow-up correspondence

3. Lawful Basis for Processing

We only process data when we have a lawful basis, such as:

Legitimate interests: Maintaining public safety and security

Legal obligation: Complying with UK data protection law

Consent: When you voluntarily provide information to us

4. How Long We Keep Data

CCTV footage: Stored for a maximum of 31 days, unless required for a verified request or investigation

DSAR records and emails: Retained for up to 12 months from the date of closure

Website logs: No personal data is stored without consent

5. Who Has Access

Access is strictly limited to authorised staff and Hosts within the UK Safety Camera Network Project. Footage is never shared or sold. We may share data with law enforcement if legally required or to protect life.

All access to footage or personal data is logged and auditable.

6. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

You have the right to:

Request access to your personal data (DSAR)

Request correction of incorrect data

Request deletion of your data (in limited cases)

Object to or restrict processing in certain circumstances

Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)

Visit the ICO website for more details.

7. Data Security

We use a combination of physical, technical, and procedural safeguards to protect your data, including:

Local encrypted storage (not cloud-based)

Access control and audit logs

Regular system reviews and DPIAs for each camera installation

8. Cookies and Website Analytics

We currently do not use tracking cookies or analytics tools on our website. If this changes in future, this policy will be updated and consent will be requested where required.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. All changes will be published on this page, and the "Last updated" date will be changed accordingly.

10. Contacting Us

When returning an item, you may be responsible for the return shipping costs unless the return is due to an error on our part, such as a defective or incorrect item sent. We recommend using a trackable shipping method and retaining the shipping receipt as proof of shipment.

Return shipping
